Cover Star Story

Driving the Future of Automotive Engineering

Dr. Maya Thompson is a mechanical engineering PhD and automotive engineer passionate about designing the future of sustainable transportation. Based in New Zealand, she has spent more than a decade working in vehicle innovation, focusing on electric vehicle systems, energy efficiency, and advanced automotive technologies. Growing up with a fascination for how machines worked, Maya pursued engineering despite being one of the few women in her classes. She later earned her PhD in Mechanical Engineering, where her research focused on improving battery performance and reducing energy waste in modern vehicles. Today, she works alongside international engineering teams to develop smarter and more environmentally friendly automotive solutions. Beyond her technical achievements, Dr. Thompson is a strong advocate for women in STEM. She regularly mentors young women interested in engineering and speaks at schools and universities across Waikato to encourage the next generation to pursue careers in science and technology. She believes that innovation becomes stronger when diverse voices are included in the conversation. As this issue's Cover Star, Dr. Maya Thompson represents the creativity, resilience, and leadership that women continue to bring to the STEM industry. Her journey is a reminder that with opportunity and support, more women can help shape the future of engineering in New Zealand and around the world.
